Cycling Research

one of my first courses in my Master's program focused solely on user research practices.

in our term-long class project, my group decided to work with urban and suburban cyclists.

initially, we planned on creating a system that would let users navigate any city or town environment, like Google Maps for cycling. we also envisioned letting users contribute to safety updates for other riders to see.

after ten weeks, our research showed that city-based commuter cyclists rarely need navigation for their daily rides.

the same applied to cyclists in the suburbs. they tended to ride for much longer periods, so they were already familiar with the routes in their respective areas.

ultimately, we found no aversion from cyclists for a cycling safety app, but potential users were unmoved by our navigation idea. we concluded that we needed to do more research to truly understand what our users might want.
