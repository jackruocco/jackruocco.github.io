General Assembly

a class at General Assembly was my introduction to doing user experience design.

the course presented an opportunity to work on real U.X. problems with real clientele.

my chosen focus? small business owners.

ultimately, i designed an app called "FirStep."

it was specifically designed to help new entrepreneurs.

users- 1099 and LLC workers- didn't need the pocket legal aid i originally thought they'd need.

they needed tools to track orders, advertise products, and communicate with customers. they also needed to stay time- and cost-efficient.

through the process of designing the app, i was also able to become adept with Adobe XD and Sketch, plus some Photoshop.

unfortunately, due to a late pivot in the design process, FirStep didn't have the benefit of undergoing competitive analysis.

however, usability testing proved that FirStep was still useful for its intended base. participant responses were highly positive. some even asked if they could test it again for fun!
